Campus and Continuity

Lessons That Stayed

What I Learned

  • Most of 12th grade unfolded during the COVID lockdown, so online classes and self-preparation became the core of my board exam journey.
  • I learned to build structured study schedules, subject-wise time blocks, and more disciplined daily routines.
  • I became more intentional about study methods, using active recall, spaced repetition, and timed practice to study more efficiently.
  • The year taught me how to organize my preparation independently and stay steady without a normal classroom environment.

Direction Became Clearer

Interests That Grew Stronger

  • Self-study made me more comfortable with analytical subjects and independent problem solving.
  • The discipline of planning, revising, and learning without constant supervision strengthened my confidence in technical study.
  • This period made engineering feel like a clearer path, especially fields that demand structured thinking and persistence.

School Highlights

Achievements / Activities

  • Studied alongside international students who were also preparing for their finals, which broadened my view of discipline and academic preparation.
  • Built connections through a student club that included peers from different countries, making networking a meaningful part of that year.
  • Learned to become more self-reliant while preparing for boards and helping others stay motivated too.
  • As a class, we pitched in together by giving contribution slips and financial support for our school cleaning workers.

Adaptation

Times of Pandemic

  • Coping with restricted socializing became part of learning how to stay balanced during a demanding academic year.
  • The lockdown period made consistency, emotional steadiness, and independent routines just as important as subject preparation.
  • That phase strengthened my ability to stay focused in unusual circumstances and keep moving forward with purpose.
